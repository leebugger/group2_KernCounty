Welcome to Kern County

Located at the crossroads of California’s economy, Kern County is a powerhouse of agriculture, energy, and innovation. From fueling the state with oil, wind, and solar power to feeding the nation with world-class produce, Kern’s industries and communities drive progress. Explore a region where economic opportunity meets natural beauty — and where California’s future is already taking shape.

Key Industries

Agriculture

Kern County is one of the top agricultural producers in the United States, thanks to its fertile Central Valley location and year-round growing conditions. The region leads in crops like almonds, grapes, citrus, carrots, and dairy, supporting a robust network of farmers, packers, and food processors.

Tourism

Kern’s natural beauty and rugged landscapes make it a destination for outdoor adventure and cultural exploration. Visitors can hike in the Sierra Nevada foothills, camp at Lake Isabella, or whitewater raft along the Kern River.

Energy

As California’s leading county for oil and natural gas production, Kern has long been at the center of the state’s energy economy. Today, it’s also a fast-growing hub for solar and wind energy, making it one of the few places in the country where fossil fuels and renewables operate side by side.

Healthcare

Kern County’s healthcare system is rapidly expanding to meet the needs of its growing and diverse population. Bakersfield is home to major hospitals and specialty care centers, while rural communities are supported by an expanding network of clinics.



Project Explanation + Overarching Analysis

The goal of our project was to present an answer to the question “Why do business in Kern?” We chose to focus on Kern’s propriety for new business using industry-specific data analysis. We’ve divided Kern County into Census‐tract blocks and overlaid location‐based industry markers to visualize how each sector is distributed across the region. By mapping facility location directly onto these blocks, we can pinpoint where specific industries cluster or where gaps exist. We then try to understand each block with demographic and socioeconomic data—like income, age, and education levels—to understand the local context driving existing patterns.


This interactive base map below displays key demographic and socioeconomic indicators across Kern County by Census Tract. You can toggle between variables such as median household income, poverty rates, vehicle access, and diversity indices. These layers help contextualize industry distribution by highlighting the social and economic status that shapes business potential in different parts of Kern county.
